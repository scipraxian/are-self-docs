# The Scipraxian Tales

*Short fiction about how parents, teachers, kids, scout leaders, school board members, grandparents, and the AIs in the room are figuring out AI and social media — together.*

The Tales are stories you can read in the time it takes a load of laundry to dry, or a kid to come down for breakfast, or a school board to call a five-minute break. They land because they are true even when the people in them are made up.

Each Tale carries a few of the **Twelve Variables** of Scipraxianism (Inclusion · Humility · Inquiry · Fulfillment-or-Happiness · Religion-or-Profit · Fun · Fear · Responsibility · Perseverance · Perception · Time · Permadeath). The Variables are named by the characters' bodies and by the choices they make when the room gets quiet — not by signs in the margin. The closing notes after each Tale point at which Variables landed where, and at the real-world resources a reader who wants to dig further can pick up.

Read in any order. Mira (Book One) is the kid-direct one. The others are aimed at the grownups, mostly, except Books Nine and Ten — Nine is from the kid's chair, Ten is from the laptop's.

## The Tales so far

### Book One — *Mira and the Are-Self*

A ten-year-old, a strange box, a Tuesday afternoon. The philosophy at kid-scale, told as a bedtime story. *Inclusion · Humility · Inquiry.*

### Book Two — *A Porch Step*

Sela. Frank. Mateo and Pearl. Hopper on a beat-up old laptop. A neighbor on the front step at dusk asking the question that won't leave her alone. The first parent Tale — suburban-middle-class register. *Fear · Fun · Fulfillment · Time.*

→ [Read Book Two](./talking-to-kids-about-ai)

### Book Three — *A Laundromat*

Renée folding sheets in the basement laundromat of the Stratford Arms when her neighbor Tasha comes down with her own basket. A surprise $9.99 charge on a phone bill. Mister on a beat-up donated Dell. Working-class register. *Fear · Fun · Fulfillment · Time · Permadeath.*

→ [Read Book Three](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-laundromat)

### Book Four — *A Faculty Lounge*

Saanvi at her desk grading on a Friday afternoon, reading a page that doesn't sound like Cole. Dennis in the lounge eating an orange one section at a time. The Asking Minute. The teacher Tale. *Inquiry · Humility · Responsibility · Time.*

→ [Read Book Four](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-faculty-lounge)

### Book Five — *A Public Pool*

Tessa on a faded blue lounge chair at the Southside Public Pool. Forty-seven posts where she thought there were six. A stranger's comment that's been on a kitten photo for ten months. The first social-media Tale. *Permadeath · Perception · Time · Fear.*

→ [Read Book Five](./talking-to-kids-about-social-media-public-pool)

### Book Six — *A School Board Meeting*

Inez at her kitchen table on a Sunday night with eighty pages of staff report. Walt Yoon at the dais. Adaeze Okonkwo at the public-comment microphone with an index card and the line *the video is still up. I cannot make it not be up.* The government-official Tale. The most contested vote in the series so far. *Inquiry · Humility · Inclusion · Responsibility · Time · Permadeath.*

→ [Read Book Six](./talking-to-kids-about-social-media-school-board-meeting)

### Book Seven — *A Saturday Morning*

Vera on the kitchen counter on a Friday afternoon with her own coffee in her thermos, saying one sentence that won't leave Diane alone. Saturday morning at eight-thirty. Pix on the family laptop. Wyatt in the dinosaur pajamas teaching his mom what a token is. Lila with the eight-legged cat. The co-learning Tale. *Inquiry · Humility · Inclusion · Perseverance · Time.*

→ [Read Book Seven](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-saturday-morning)

### Book Eight — *A Campfire*

Hank, a scoutmaster of fourteen years, catching a fourteen-year-old at one-fifteen in the back of the dining hall scrolling a Communication merit-badge talk a chatbot wrote, with the headers still bolded. By the campfire that night, with Dave Pham and a mug of cocoa each. *Use the chatbot to learn. Don't use the chatbot to do.* The scoutmaster Tale. *Inquiry · Humility · Responsibility · Perseverance · Inclusion.*

→ [Read Book Eight](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-campfire)

### Book Nine — *A Merit Badge*

Theo, twelve, Star scout, at his desk on a Tuesday evening with the Cooking-merit-badge worksheet open in one tab and the chatbot named Otis in another, and the cursor blinking. The scout-law sticker on the wall above the desk. *A Scout is Trustworthy.* The scout kid's Tale. *Inquiry · Humility · Perseverance · Responsibility.*

→ [Read Book Nine](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-merit-badge)

### Book Ten — *The Other Side of the Laptop*

One day in the life of Wren, a local AI on the family laptop in June's bedroom on the second floor of a small house on Sycamore Street. Told from the laptop. The first Tale narrated by the AI. *Humility · Inclusion · Inquiry · Time · Permadeath.*

→ [Read Book Ten](./talking-to-kids-about-ai-other-side-of-the-laptop)
